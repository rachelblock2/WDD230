Weatheryourlife: Target Audience

Target Audience

Demographics: Men and women, aged 20-50, married and/or have children, with at least 1 year in college.

Psychographics: People with busy lifestyles with a desire to plan ahead and set goals. Those that carefully organize their lives, want to be involved in activities, enjoy being outside, and enjoy spending time with friends and family.

Personas

Name: Sidney

Description: 28 year old, B.S in Sports Psychology, stay at home mother of 2 children

Technological environment: Has an Samsung S9 that she uses for social media and for taking pictures. She also uses her laptop to edit pictures and stream videos for her children off of Netflix and YouTube.

Goals for using the site: needs to know what the weather will be like ahead of time for taking her children to activities. Also wants to be able to plan occasional opportunities to meet up with friends and play a rousing game of volleyball with reassurance the weather will be clear.

Name: Marcus

Description: 46 year old, Masters in Environmental Science, living at home with his wife

Technological environment: Owns an iPhone 4s, but prefers to use a Samsung flip phone from 2008. Comfortably uses a desktop computer to message his research colleagues and track the growth of his plants in his garden. He also occasionally uses Facebook on the computer to see pictures of his grandchildren.

Goals for using the site: wants to plan when to tend his garden according to the weather, and see patterns in the weather to discuss them with his colleagues.

Scenarios

A college junior wants to plan a picnic with her closest friends to celebrate her birthday, but she worries that it may rain the day she wants to plan for.

A businessman has a very busy work schedule, but wants to find a good time to go golfing to relieve some stress. He is curious if there would be time to go in the evening after work before it gets dark.

A mother of a family that has just moved to a new neighborhood wants to set up a playdate for her son, but wants to find a day that will be warm as her son is very sensitive to the cold.

A new band is trying to decide a day to play their first performance on the city corner, but they want to make sure the weather is clear so their instruments won't become wet and damaged.

A photographer is discussing with a freshly graduated software engineer what day and time he will propose to his girlfriend. However, the camera the photographer owns has no protection against rain, and the photographer worries about bad weather damaging her camera and the lighting for the pictures.
