Bayer’s phosphate mine company in Idaho agrees to clean up waste to settle lawsuit

From wire and staff reports

BOISE, Idaho — A phosphate mining company this month reached a toxic-waste cleanup agreement with the Environmental Protection Agency and the Shoshone-Bannock Tribes over mining waste in southeastern Idaho. Officials with P4 Production LLC, a subsidiary of Bayer’s Monsanto Co., said in a statement on March 4 that they worked with the tribes and EPA to develop the remediation plan for the Ballard Mine near Soda Springs. “We fully support this plan and will continue to work collaboratively and transparently throughout this process,” the company wrote in an email to The Associated Press. “As members of the Soda Springs community, we have great respect for the people and the environment in this historic and beautiful area of Southeast Idaho.”
